For a solo renter, co-living is usually **cheaper than taking an entire 1 BHK**—often by 30–40% versus furnished flats when you factor brokerage and setup. Against a **traditional PG**, the gap narrows: budget PGs in outer belts can still undercut branded co-living on headline rent.

Co-living wins on **predictable all-in pricing**, zero brokerage, and faster move-in. PGs can win on absolute rupees if you accept fewer amenities and less standardisation. Splitting a 2 BHK with a roommate is the main alternative that competes on per-person cost while offering more privacy.

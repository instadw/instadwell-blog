## 1. Location First: Why Proximity Matters More Than Rent

One of the biggest mistakes first-time movers make is choosing a PG purely based on price.

Living close to your office or college:

-   Saves 1–2 hours of daily commute
    
-   Reduces burnout and transport costs
    
-   Improves work-life balance
    
-   Makes short-term stays more manageable

## PG vs Coliving: What’s the Real Difference?

| Feature      | Traditional PG | Coliving Space           |
| ------------ | -------------- | ------------------------ |
| Room Type    | Mostly shared  | Shared & private         |
| Furnishing   | Basic          | Fully furnished          |
| Rules        | Often strict   | More flexible            |
| Maintenance  | Inconsistent   | Professionally managed   |
| Community    | Limited        | Designed for interaction |
| Transparency | Varies         | Generally clearer        |

Coliving works better for professionals and long-term stays, while PGs may suit short-term or budget-focused needs.

## 4. Safety Is Non-Negotiable

Safety should never be treated as optional.

Must-have basics:

-   CCTV in common areas
    
-   Secure entry systems
    
-   Power backup
    
-   Stable internet (critical for WFH & online classes)
    

If a property avoids answering safety questions clearly, that’s a red flag.

----------

## 5. Common Red Flags Renters in Pune Warn About

Based on repeated renter experiences:

### Hidden Charges

-   Electricity & water billed separately
    
-   Exit or notice-period penalties
    
-   Maintenance fees added later
    

### Misleading Practices

-   Showing a model room, not the actual vacant one
    
-   Verbal promises not mentioned in the agreement
    

Always see the exact room you’ll be staying in.

----------

## 6. Real Problems You Should Know Before Moving to a PG or Coliving in Pune

Water & Electricity Issues

-   Many societies depend on tanker water, even when “corporation water” is promised
    
-   Hard water & hair loss concerns (common in East Pune)
    
-   Summer months (March–June) often mean water timing restrictions
    
-   Power cuts still occur in some outskirts despite claims of backup (especially during peak summer)
    

Hygiene & Pest Problems

-   Bed bugs are common in older PGs with frequent tenant turnover
    
-   Kitchens and water purifiers are often poorly maintained
    
-   Washrooms may look clean during visits but degrade quickly
    

Distance vs Reality

-   “10 minutes from Hinjewadi” can actually mean 45 minutes during peak hours
    
-   Maps don’t account for traffic choke points
    
-   Pune's traffic can turn a “10-minute distance” into a 45-minute commute. Choose your location wisely.
    

Agreement Gaps

-   Rules change after moving in
    
-   Refund delays during exit
    
-   House rules enforced verbally, not in writing
    

Knowing these problems upfront helps you ask better questions before booking.

----------

## 7. Hygiene: The Silent Deal Breaker

Hygiene issues rarely show up in listing photos—but they matter long-term.

Check during a visit:

-   Washroom cleanliness
    
-   Water quality and storage
    
-   Pest-control schedule
    
-   Frequency of room & common-area cleaning
    

If hygiene is unclear, expect extra food, medical, and relocation expenses later.

----------

## 8. Rules, Flexibility & Daily Comfort

Many renters struggle with:

-   Strict curfews
    
-   Visitor restrictions
    
-   No flexibility for replacements or early exit
    

If you’re:

-   On a short-term contract
    
-   Interning
    
-   Exploring job opportunities
    

Flexibility matters more than luxury.

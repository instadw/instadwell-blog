## 1. Uncapped Mobility: What It Actually Means

The January 27, 2026, agreement commits the EU to "uncapped mobility" for Indian students.

Uncapped does not mean unregulated. While the EU won't place a "hard cap" on the total number of Indians allowed in, you still need to meet the standard criteria. Individual vetting remains strict.

-   The Requirement: You still need university admission, health insurance, and proof of funds (typically €11,000 - €15,000/year).
    
-   The "Win": It protects Indian students from sudden policy shifts (like those seen in Canada) where total student numbers are slashed overnight.
    

----------

## 2. The Death of the Passport Sticker (Schengen Digitalization)

A major part of the 2026 roadmap is the continued digitalization of the EU visa process for Indian students.

-   Move Toward Digital Visas: The EU is transitioning toward electronic visas and online applications, reducing reliance on paper documents and in-person visits. However, physical visa formats and national procedures still apply in many cases.

-   Biometric Passports: Students are encouraged to use biometric (e-passports), as these integrate more smoothly with the EU’s automated border systems.
    
-   Digital Border Controls: Under the new Entry/Exit System (EES), manual passport stamping is being phased out. Entry and exit will be recorded digitally using facial recognition and fingerprints at automated border gates.
    
----------

## 3. A Standardized Path for Post-Study Work

Post-study work options in Europe for Indian students are becoming more predictable.

- The Framework Goal: The agreement encourages Member States to offer up to 3-year post-study work permits for Master’s and PhD graduates, where national laws allow.

- The Baseline: Most countries provide a 9–12 month job-seeker residence window after graduation to find qualifying employment.

### FAQs: Your Questions Answered

Is EU-India mobility uncapped for Indian students?

Yes, in terms of total numbers. There is no maximum "quota" for Indian students. However, you must still pass the individual visa interview and meet financial requirements.

How much proof of funds is required to study in Europe?

For 2026, most EU member states require between €11,000 and €15,000 per year in a blocked account or via a notarized financial guarantee.

Is the 3-year post-study work guaranteed in all EU countries?

It is the official target of the 2026 Mobility Pact, but implementation varies by country. Currently, Germany and France are the most aligned with this 3-year goal.

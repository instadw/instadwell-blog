## PG Rent in Gurgaon: What to Expect in 2026

Gurgaon PG rent varies sharply by sector, room type, and whether food is included. Broadly:

| Room Type | Monthly Rent Range |
|---|---|
| Triple sharing | Rs. 6,000 to Rs. 9,000 |
| Double sharing | Rs. 8,000 to Rs. 14,000 |
| Single room | Rs. 12,000 to Rs. 22,000 |
| Premium / co-living | Rs. 15,000 to Rs. 35,000 |

Food (two meals a day) typically adds Rs. 2,500 to Rs. 4,500 per month on top of rent. Some operators bundle it; others treat it as optional.

If you are targeting a **PG in Gurgaon under 5,000**, that is very difficult in 2026 for anything near the employment hubs. You will find some options in outer sectors like 45, 46, and Subhash Chowk, but expect triple-sharing rooms and a longer commute.

## Best Areas for PG in Gurgaon (by Work Location)

### Near Cyber City and DLF Phase 2/3

Cyber City is the single densest office cluster in Gurgaon. PG near Cyber City Gurgaon is in highest demand, and rent reflects that. Sectors 24, 25, and 26 are walkable or a short auto ride away. Expect to pay Rs. 10,000 to Rs. 18,000 for a decent double-sharing room here.

### Udyog Vihar (Phase 1 to 5)

Udyog Vihar is the older industrial-cum-tech belt. It houses MNCs, BPOs, and manufacturing units. PG in Udyog Vihar Gurgaon is dense and reasonably priced compared to Cyber City. Sectors 21 and 14 are the closest residential pockets.

### Sectors 43, 44, 45, 48, 49

These mid-Gurgaon sectors are popular for people working in Golf Course Road companies. Good transport connectivity to both Cyber City and MG Road. Rent is slightly more affordable than sectors directly adjacent to Cyber City.

### IFFCO Chowk and MG Road

Central location with good metro access. PG near IFFCO Chowk Gurgaon works well if your office is anywhere on the Yellow Line corridor.

### Huda City Centre and Sector 18

The southern end of the NH-48 corridor. PG near Huda City Centre is popular with professionals working in Ambience Mall offices or the tech parks in Sector 18.

## PG With Food vs Without Food in Gurgaon

**With food:** Saves time and is usually cost-effective if you are not eating out regularly. Most managed PGs offer two meals (breakfast and dinner). Lunch is rarely included. Check the menu variety before committing, as monotonous food is the most common complaint across reviews.

**Without food:** More flexibility. Works well if your office has a subsidised canteen or if you prefer cooking. Most independent PGs in Gurgaon fall in this category.

Many professionals specifically want to decouple food from rent. If that matters to you, look at Zolo properties where food is an add-on rather than bundled.

## Is PG Cheaper Than a Hostel in Gurgaon?

Short answer: it depends on what you are comparing.

A dormitory-style hostel in Gurgaon runs Rs. 5,000 to Rs. 8,000 per month but offers very little privacy. A PG at the same price point will give you a shared room with 2 to 3 occupants in a residential building.

For stays longer than three months, a PG is almost always more practical and often cheaper on a per-amenity basis than a hostel. Hostels in Gurgaon are mostly designed for short-term or transient stays.

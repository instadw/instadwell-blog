## PG vs flat in Indore

A PG usually wins on upfront cost. Most verified options on this list ask for one to two months of rent as deposit, while an independent flat typically demands a larger security deposit plus your own furniture, appliances and a broker fee. A shared room at Rs. 5000 to 9000 includes housekeeping, RO water and often meals, none of which come standard with a bare flat. The trade off is privacy and space, a flat gives you a full kitchen and living area, a shared room gives you a bed and shared common spaces. Most students and early career professionals start in a PG and consider a flat only once they have a fixed group of flatmates in mind.

## Students vs working professionals, who should pick what

Students weighing coaching schedules and college hours generally do better close to Prestige, Vaishnav or Acropolis, where triple sharing rooms in the Rs. 5000 to 7000 range keep costs down and walking distance removes the commute question entirely. Working professionals commuting to TCS, Infosys or the C21 Business Park offices tend to prioritise single or double occupancy with AC, since a longer workday makes a quiet, private room worth the extra Rs. 3000 to 5000 a month. If your office allows hybrid work, a slightly cheaper room in Bhanwar Kua or Mahalaxmi Nagar with a longer commute is usually a better trade than paying a premium to stay central.

## Safety tips before you book

Never pay a full deposit before physically visiting the room or verifying it through a call with the resident manager. Ask for the exact floor, sharing type and whether AC and electricity are billed separately before you commit. If you are booking for a woman, confirm the warden's presence in person, not just in the listing description. Confirm the notice period and deposit refund policy in writing, verbally agreed terms are the most common source of disputes at move out.

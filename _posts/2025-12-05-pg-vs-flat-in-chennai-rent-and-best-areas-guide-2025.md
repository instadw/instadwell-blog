## PG vs Flat in Chennai: The Honest Cost Breakdown

| Factor | PG (shared, food included) | 1BHK Flat |
|---|---|---|
| Monthly rent | Rs. 10,000-15,000 (OMR) | Rs. 20,000-30,000 (OMR) |
| Deposit required | 1-2 months | 6-10 months (often Rs. 1.5L-3L) |
| Food | Included (variable quality) | Self-cook or Swiggy |
| Electricity | Often included or capped | Separate bill, Rs. 1,500-3,000+/month |
| Water | Managed by owner | Tanker dependency in OMR |
| Maintenance | Owner handles | Your problem |
| Lock-in period | 1-3 months | 11-month agreement standard |
| Move-in cost (total) | Rs. 20,000-30,000 | Rs. 1,50,000-3,00,000+ |

**The deposit reality:** Tamil Nadu law caps deposits at 3 months' rent. In practice, landlords in OMR and Sholinganallur routinely ask for 10 months and settle at 5-6 after negotiation. Always insist on a written rental agreement documenting the deposit amount and refund terms before paying anything.

**The hidden flat costs:** Residents who switched from PG to flat consistently report underestimating the real cost. You lose food, gain cooking time, and start paying separately for electricity, water (tanker in OMR), Wi-Fi, and maintenance. The all-inclusive PG math often wins for the first 6-12 months in a new city.

**When a flat makes sense:** If you're staying more than a year, have 2-3 friends to split rent, want kitchen access, or value independence over convenience, a flat works out. But the move-in capital required is steep.

## Area-by-Area PG Guide

### OMR and Sholinganallur

The primary IT corridor for TCS, Wipro, Cognizant, Infosys, and a cluster of mid-size tech firms. Shared PG rent runs Rs. 10,000-15,000 with food included. A 1BHK in the same stretch costs Rs. 20,000-30,000, plus the deposit hurdle. The MRTS line (Perungudi station) makes the inner-city commute manageable, though the stretch beyond Sholinganallur toward Siruseri depends on company buses or cabs.

One practical warning most listings won't mention: water supply is a persistent problem along OMR. Most buildings rely on tanker water, not metro water. This applies to PGs and flats equally. Ask specifically whether the property has metro water connection or runs on tankers before committing.

For a full list of verified PGs in this corridor, see our [OMR and Sholinganallur PG guide](https://instadwell.com/blog/best-pg-omr-chennai-sholinganallur-karapakkam-siruseri-guide-2026).

### Velachery

One of Chennai's strongest PG markets because it sits between the OMR IT corridor and central Chennai, giving residents access to both without committing to either. Shared PG accommodation in Velachery runs Rs. 9,000-13,000/month with food; single-room options start at Rs. 15,000-20,000.

The area draws IT professionals working in Perungudi and Taramani, students at nearby colleges, and working women specifically because of MRTS connectivity combined with walkable shopping centres, hospitals, and restaurants. Ladies PGs in Velachery are consistently in demand for exactly this reason: good transport plus commercial infrastructure in one locality.

### Taramani

Taramani sits adjacent to the IITM Research Park and several IT campuses, making it a quieter alternative to Sholinganallur for professionals who want shorter commutes without OMR congestion. Shared PG rent is Rs. 8,000-12,000. MRTS Taramani station connects toward Chennai Beach and Velachery. Less commercially developed than Velachery, but noticeably cheaper.

### Adyar

The preferred base for IIT Madras and Anna University students, as well as professionals who want a greener, less congested neighbourhood. Shared PG rent is Rs. 9,000-14,000. Adyar is considered one of the safer and more walkable parts of Chennai, and the Adyar MRTS station connects north and south. Female residents specifically rate Adyar well for day-to-day comfort.

### T Nagar

Central Chennai's commercial hub. If your office or college is in or near the city centre, T Nagar gives you bus connectivity to most of Chennai without the commute drag of OMR. Shared PG rent is Rs. 8,500-13,000. The area is dense and noisy but extremely well connected.

### Porur and Ramanujan IT Park Area

Porur serves the Ramanujan IT Park cluster as well as companies along the inner ring road. Shared PG rent is Rs. 7,500-11,000, partly because public transport options are thinner. Most residents rely on company buses or two-wheelers. If your office provides transport, this is a cost-effective base.

### Anna Nagar

Premium, well-planned, and noticeably more expensive than most other areas. Shared PG rent starts at Rs. 10,000-16,000. Phase 2 metro connectivity has improved access. Recommended if budget allows and you want residential quality over proximity to any single IT hub.

### Tambaram and GST Road

The college corridor. SRM, MCC, Vels, and a cluster of engineering colleges sit along this stretch, and PG rents reflect the student budget: Rs. 6,500-9,000 for shared rooms. Suburban rail connects Tambaram directly to Chennai Central, which is a genuine advantage for students who travel home on weekends. For PG options along this stretch, see our [GST Road and Pallavaram PG guide](https://instadwell.com/blog/best-pg-gst-road-pallavaram-urapakkam-chennai-2026).

### PG in Nungambakkam: Is the Premium Worth It?

Nungambakkam is one of the most expensive PG markets in Chennai, with shared rooms running Rs. 12,000-18,000/month. The premium is driven by central location, Nungambakkam Railway Station access, quick connectivity to Anna Salai and T Nagar, and walkable commercial infrastructure.

For professionals working in finance, consulting, media, or central Chennai offices, paying higher rent here often saves more in commute time than the price difference suggests. It is also consistently rated well by female residents for day-to-day comfort, given its cosmopolitan character and commercial density.

## Electricity Billing in Chennai PGs: What to Watch For

This is a recurring issue across Chennai PGs and worth understanding before you sign anything.

Standard residential TNEB tariffs run approximately Rs. 4-6 per unit for moderate consumption. Many PG owners bill tenants at Rs. 9-14 per unit, citing commercial connection rates. Some of that is legitimate (commercial lines do cost more), but a portion is margin the owner pockets. One resident reported being billed Rs. 14/unit in Ramapuram with no breakdown provided.

Three things to check before moving in:

**AC billing:** Chennai heat means AC runs for 6-8 months of the year. AC usage adds Rs. 1,500-3,000/month to your bill, sometimes more with older units. Ask specifically what the per-unit rate is and whether AC is sub-metered separately.

**Bimonthly billing cycle:** Tamil Nadu runs a bimonthly electricity billing cycle. This means your PG owner receives one bill covering two months but may collect monthly from you. Ask for a copy of the actual TNEB bill when you pay.

**Power backup scope:** Ask whether backup covers AC or only lights and fan. Many PGs advertise power backup but it only runs fans and common lights, not room ACs.

## Food in Chennai PGs: The Honest Picture

Most PGs in Chennai serve predominantly South Indian food: idli, dosa, rice, sambar, rasam. North Indian dishes are offered by some PGs near OMR and Velachery, but reviews are consistently mixed. The common experience is monotonous menus with low protein content. One resident who paid Rs. 12,000 for a food-included PG reported spending an additional Rs. 5,000/month on outside food anyway.

If North Indian food is important to you, the realistic options are: order via Swiggy/Zomato regularly (adds Rs. 3,000-5,000/month), find a North Indian tiffin service or mess near your PG (cheaper and more consistent), or choose a PG without food and manage independently.

PGs without food are available in most areas at Rs. 1,500-2,500 less per month than food-included options. If you plan to rely on delivery apps regardless, a no-food PG is often the smarter budget choice.

For women relocating to Chennai, the safety picture is nuanced. Chennai is generally considered safer than several other Indian metros, but street harassment and staring are reported as common experiences, particularly in less commercial areas and on crowded public transport.

Areas rated most comfortable by female residents: Adyar, Besant Nagar, Nungambakkam, Anna Nagar. These are more cosmopolitan in character and have better-lit streets and more walkable commercial strips. OMR has strong female PG supply but is more isolated at night.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**What is the average PG rent in Chennai in 2026?**

For a shared room (2-3 sharing) with food included, expect Rs. 10,000-15,000/month in OMR and Sholinganallur, and Rs. 8,500-13,000 in Velachery, T Nagar, and Taramani. Tambaram and GST Road are cheaper at Rs. 6,500-9,000.

**How much deposit do Chennai landlords ask for?**

Flat landlords typically ask for 6-10 months' rent, though Tamil Nadu law caps it at 3 months. Negotiating to 4-6 months is common in practice. PG operators ask for 1-2 months. This deposit gap is one of the strongest practical reasons to start with a PG.

**Are Chennai PGs good for people from North India?**

The city is welcoming but the PG food situation is a genuine challenge. Most PGs serve South Indian food primarily. North Indian residents typically supplement with tiffin services or delivery apps. Plan for an additional Rs. 3,000-5,000/month in food costs if the PG menu doesn't suit you.

**Is Chennai safe for women in PGs?**

Generally yes, especially in Adyar, Besant Nagar, Anna Nagar, and Nungambakkam. OMR has strong female PG supply with security features. Check for CCTV, biometric entry, and a clear gate-close policy before booking. See our [women's PG guide](https://instadwell.com/blog/best-pgs-for-women-in-chennai-2026-ultimate-safety-comfort-guide) for area-specific options.

**What is the typical lock-in period for a Chennai PG?**

Most PGs have a 1-3 month lock-in. Flats require an 11-month rental agreement with 1-2 months' notice. If you're uncertain about your stay length, a PG gives you significantly more flexibility.

**PG or flat for a fresher joining their first job in Chennai?**

PG for the first 6-12 months, almost always. The move-in cost difference is large, the city is unfamiliar, and the community in a PG eases the adjustment. Reassess after you know the city and have colleagues to split a flat with.

**Which area is best for PG accommodation in Chennai?**

For IT professionals: Sholinganallur, Perungudi, Taramani, and Velachery. For students: Adyar and Tambaram. For central Chennai roles: Nungambakkam and T Nagar offer the shortest commutes.

**What does a single room PG cost in Chennai?**

Single room PGs start at Rs. 15,000-20,000/month depending on locality and amenities. Shared accommodation remains the most cost-effective option at Rs. 8,000-15,000/month.

**Which areas have the cheapest PGs in Chennai?**

Tambaram, GST Road, Porur, and outer Sholinganallur offer the lowest rents, with shared rooms from Rs. 6,500-9,000/month.

**Which area is best for IT professionals looking for PGs in Chennai?**

Sholinganallur, Perungudi, Taramani, Velachery, and Siruseri are the most popular because they sit closest to Chennai's major IT parks and tech corridors.

**What are the best PG areas for men in Chennai?**

For men relocating for work, Sholinganallur, Velachery, Taramani, Porur, and Perungudi offer the strongest combination of affordability, commute convenience, and managed PG supply. Shared accommodation runs Rs. 8,000-15,000/month. Most PGs in these corridors offer food-included options.

**Are unisex PGs available in Chennai?**

Yes. Co-living operators and managed PG brands across OMR, Velachery, Perungudi, and Nungambakkam offer unisex accommodation, typically with separate floors or wing-based access controls for men and women.
